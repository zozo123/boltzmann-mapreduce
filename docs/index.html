Uncertainty-aware reduction

Parallel answers are cheap. Knowing what to trust is the hard part.

Make every worker return an estimate, its uncertainty, and where its evidence came from. Then combine independent results without pretending every answer deserves an equal vote.

The idea in one glance

Keep the information that ordinary reducers throw away.

A scalar says what a worker thinks. A statistical result also says how much evidence supports it and whether that evidence was already counted.

Why this is valuable

Not a smarter vote. A more honest interface.

The reducer becomes useful because it preserves three facts that a mean or majority vote cannot express.

01

Evidence earns weight

A precise estimate contributes more than a noisy one through total precision Pₖ = nₖJₖ—not through worker identity or verbal confidence.

02

Merging scales as a tree

The canonical summary is associative and commutative. Reduce locally, stream incrementally, or combine across a fanout tree without changing the answer.

03

Conflict stays visible

The product normalizer contains a disagreement term. Confident workers that point in different directions leave an explicit audit signal.

The scientific boundary

Isolation makes workers separate. It does not make their evidence independent.

This distinction is the most important part of the paper. Multiplication is justified only when workers estimate the same target from independent, non-overlapping evidence.

✓

Use it when

Worker summaries have a defensible uncertainty model and genuinely distinct evidence.

  • One shared parameter and compatible units
  • Disjoint evidence or explicitly modeled dependence
  • Valid Gaussian or local-asymptotic approximation
×

Do not use it as

A generic trust score for correlated agents, adversarial workers, or different local truths.

  • Self-reported model confidence
  • An automatic Byzantine defense
  • Proof that separate sandboxes are independent
Core warningShared prompts, models, repositories, tests, or fork ancestors create common-mode error. Provenance is necessary; it is not yet a complete correlation model.

Auditable artifact

Small enough to inspect. Complete enough to rerun.

The repository checks the Gaussian algebra, exact normalizer, hierarchical merge, schema, overlap guard, and three deterministic statistical paths. Platform logs are execution observations—not a cross-provider benchmark.
